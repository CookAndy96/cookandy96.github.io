Area of Research

My research area focuses on galaxy evolution in computational simulations. Specifically, I study the physical and observable properties of the circumgalactic medium - the gaseous component of dark matter haloes - around galaxies. I look to understand how the CGM changes around different mass galaxies and in different physical conditions (e.g. magnetic fields, cosmic rays, etc.).

Physical and Observable Properties of the Circumgalactic Medium

The CGM is a complex, multiphase region meaning the gas spans a wide range of temperatures. In this research, we focused on analysing the dependence of the physical properties, including temperature, density, metallicity and radial velocity, and observable properties, such as column density, with the halo mass. This publication can be found at the following link https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2024arXiv240905578C/abstract

Impact of Enhanced Volumetric Refinement in Circumgalactic Medium Simulations

One of the major advancements in computational simulations of the circumgalactic medium is additional volumetric refinement to the size of gas cells. This improves the resolution of the simulations beyond what mass resolution can give us. Here, we focused on comparing the effects of enhanced spatial refinement in two haloes, 1011M&solar and 1012M&solar.
